\## Research Summary -what I have done so far?(Q\&A)



\*\*P1 — Vortex-emergence efficiency in the kagome XY model\*\*

\*\*Q:\*\* **What happens when information density is at its maximum**?

\*\*A:\*\* η(T) = birth(T)/√M\_I(T) — the ratio of vortex-birth rate to collective information density — reaches its sharpest peak at a size-independent temperature, T\_η = 1.1789 ± 0.0001, confirmed via finite-size scaling (L=48–256) and bootstrap resampling.



\*\*P2 — A local restoring-strength observable bridging energy and information\*\*

\*\*Q:\*\* **What links energy dissipation to vortex formation, and is collective information reducible to any single vortex property**?

\*\*A:\*\* A local observable, "drop" (local restoring strength), mediates the Energy → Birth causal chain (correlates strongly with helicity modulus and √M\_I). However, collective information density is \*not\* reducible to any single vortex's local drop value (micro-to-macro correlation ≈ 0) — information remains an emergent, collective property of the lattice.



\*\*P3 — Waveform-dependent information transfer under external driving\*\*

\*\*Q:\*\* **Does a driven lattice discriminate the temporal structure of a perturbation, independent of the energy it injects**?

\*\*A:\*\* Yes. White noise absorbs the most energy of three canonical waveforms yet transfers statistically indistinguishable-from-zero information (net transfer entropy ≈ 0). Telegraph and sine driving absorb statistically indistinguishable energy (p=0.15) but are cleanly separated by transfer entropy (p=0.024): energy alone cannot explain this distinction, but transfer entropy does.



\*\*P4 — **Hierarchical structure of lattice responses under external driving** (P4-A); **spatial-pattern reading** (P4-B, in progress)\*\*



\*\*Q:\*\* When a driven lattice's observables are examined across many kinds of driving (different waveforms, driving power, and spatial patterns), do they all respond in the same way?

\*\*A:\*\* No. Observables split into two robust groups regardless of which axis of driving is varied. Intensity statistics (absorbed energy, vortex-birth rate, information-field peak and mean, and their temporal fluctuation) respond readily — they distinguish waveform identity, activate at low driving power, and fluctuate detectably over time. Spatial-organization statistics (correlation length, number of connected components, largest-component fraction) remain comparatively robust across every manipulation tested, including a spatially-resolved comparison (uniform vs. checkerboard driving) that recovers the same split along an independent axis.



\*\*Q (P4-B, in progress):\*\* Beyond simple periodicity, does the lattice also read more structured spatial patterns — phase gradients, phase winding, or handedness?

\*\*A (preliminary):\*\* Periodicity (checkerboard) produces the strongest spatial-pattern effect; phase-winding patterns (spiral, vortex-like) produce a smaller but still statistically robust effect, distinguishable from checkerboard and, once the spiral's radial phase gradient is made strong enough, from pure angular winding as well. Reversing the winding direction (a mirror-image, chirality test) produces no detectable difference in any observable tested — no evidence of intrinsic lattice handedness.
